About

Kalpana Chawla (March 17, 1962 – February 1, 2003) was an Indian-American astronaut and aerospace engineer who was the first woman of Indian origin to fly to space. She first flew on the Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a mission specialist and robotic arm operator aboard STS-87. Her role in the flight caused some controversy due to the failed deployment of the Shuttle-Pointed Autonomous Research Tool for Astronomy ("Spartan") module

Missions


STS-87 (1997): First spaceflight; mission specialist.

  • STS-107 (2003): Second flight; tragically died during re-entry.

    • Achievements

    • NASA Space Flight Medal

    • Congressional Space Medal of Honor (Posthumous)

    • Over 31 days in space

    • Several schools, satellites, and awards named after her

    Legacy

    Kalpana’s story continues to inspire millions of students and future astronauts. Her journey from Karnal to the stars stands as a beacon of perseverance and courage .
